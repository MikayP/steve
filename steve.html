The furniture that passes the test can display an ASTM sticker which may give parents a false sense of security that they could open more than one drawer at a time.

The tests result in recommendations that are rarely adopted by furniture makers because they cannot compete on price unless there is a level playing field. For the sake of a children's safety, we ask that the CPSC write mandatory regulations for the whole industry.
ASTM Int'l., the Consumer Product Safety Commission, Consumer Reports and Underwriters Laboratories conduct tip-over tests on medium-height chests on which a 50-lb. weight is added to one partially-extended drawer.
HinesLab conducted dresser tip-over tests on the IKEA's tallest Malm-6 chest, with the KidSafe™ baseboard, with all drawers fully extended.  There was no sign of tipping, even with 182 lbs. added.
